Services

  • Comprehensive impact and vulnerability assessments

    • Analysis of current climate change science information for local region.
    • Assessment of vulnerability to predicted climate stressors and identification of potential high impact areas.
    • Work directly with local stakeholders to identify and prioritize response options in order to increase community resilience and decrease vulnerability to climate change and extreme weather.

  • Topical vulnerability assessments

    • Detailed assessments of key areas of vulnerability
    • Physical impacts: sea-level rise, drought, extreme heat, flooding, agriculture impacts, ecological changes
    • Social impacts: emergency response capability, critical infrastructure assessment, human health impacts, climate refugees, cultural resources

  • Community climate preparedness planning

    • Work directly with community members to facilitate a conversation on how to plan for and prepare for climate change in the community.
    • Bring together stakeholders and representatives from relevant community groups to discuss climate risks, prepare for climate impacts, and build capacity to deal with extreme weather and climate related emergencies.

  • Scenario based planning assistance

    • Work with local decision-makers and planners to develop and evaluate different climate scenarios. Combining scenario based planning with risk tolerance can help the community decide how to prioritize investing their limited resources in preparing for climate change.

  • Applying for additional funding

    • Complete grant applications to fund adaptation and climate preparedness efforts.
    • Coordinate the implementation of the grant and all grant reporting requirements.

Team

Liz Rohlich

Liz has a Bachelors degree in international economics and a Masters' degree in nursing. She has worked in health care in: Austin, Texas; Homer, Alaska; and rural Bolivia. She worked in Bolivia as part of the Peace Corps and has traveled throughout Latin America and Europe. Her primary focus is on assessing and decreasing the human health impacts of climate change.

Alexander "Sascha" Petersen

Active in the climate change field since 2006, Sascha worked closely with the Climate Impacts Group at the University of Washington; the City of Seattle; and King County, Washington to identify the coastal zone impacts of sea-level rise and assess the effectiveness of policy response options.

He also worked as part of the Climate Protection Program at the City of Austin, Texas to begin evaluating the human health impacts of climate change and set goals for climate preparedness in a variety of areas from drought and flood planning to emergency response and climate refugee support. He has worked with ICLEI to develop a national sustainability index as well as a tool kit for climate change adaptation. Sascha has a Bachelors degree in physics and a Masters' degree focusing on climate change science and policy.
