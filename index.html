Services

  • Comprehensive impact and vulnerability assessments

    • Analyze current climate change science.
    • Conduct vulnerability assessments and identify potential high impact areas.
    • Collaborate with stakeholder to prioritize response options for increasing community resilience.

  • Topical vulnerability assessments

    • Physical impacts: sea-level rise, drought, extreme heat, flooding, agriculture impacts, ecological changes.
    • Social impacts: emergency response capability, critical infrastructure assessment, human health impacts, climate refugees, cultural resources.

  • Community climate preparedness planning

    • Facilitate community conversations on planning and preparing for climate change.
    • Discuss climate risks, prepare for climate impacts, and build capacity to deal with extreme weather and climate related emergencies.

  • Scenario based planning assistance

    • Partner with local decision-makers to evaluate climate scenarios. Use scenarios and community risk tolerance to prioritize climate change preparation.

  • Applying for additional funding

    • Coordinate proposal and implementation of grant applications to fund adaptation and climate preparedness efforts.

Founders

Liz Rohlich

Liz has a Bachelor's degree in international economics and a Master's degree in nursing. She has worked in health care in: Austin, Texas; Homer, Alaska; and rural Bolivia. She worked in Bolivia as part of the Peace Corps and has traveled throughout Latin America and Europe. Her primary focus is on assessing and limiting the human health impacts of climate change.

Alexander "Sascha" Petersen

Active in the climate change field since 2006, Sascha worked closely with the Climate Impacts Group at the University of Washington; the City of Seattle; and King County, Washington to identify the coastal zone impacts of sea-level rise and assess the effectiveness of policy response options.

He also worked as part of the Climate Protection Program at the City of Austin, Texas to begin evaluating the human health impacts of climate change and set goals for climate preparedness in a variety of areas from drought and flood planning to emergency response and climate refugee support. He has worked with ICLEI to develop a national sustainability index as well as a tool kit for climate change adaptation. Sascha has a Bachelor's degree in physics and a Master's degree focusing on climate change science and policy.
